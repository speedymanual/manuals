Honda XR/XL 4-Stroke Dirt Bike Service Manuals


Like Honda's motocross bikes, their XR line of trail bikes also has its beginnings in the 1970s. The line began with small motor bikes such as the XR75 and XR80, and other models were quickly added throughout the 1980s and 1990s. While there were many different models with different size motors and features, the one thing they all had in common was the reliable air-cooled 4-stroke motors. For the most part the XR line were dedicated off-road bikes, but several models like the XR250L, XL350R and XR650L were dual-sport or "enduro" models meant for on or off road use. The smaller models did well for Honda, but they really impressed off-road riders in 1996 with the release of the XR400R. With plenty of power while still being reliable, this model remains popular today. Its motor was even borrowed and put into use in the TRX400ex sport ATV. The XR line has been discontinued in favor of the CRF line with the exception of one model, the dual-sport XR650L. It is still made without updates but its buyers don't seem to care becuase of its power and versatility.
