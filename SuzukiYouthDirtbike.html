Suzuki Youth Dirt Bike Service Manuals


Suzuki has been part of the youth dirt bike scene since the 1970s. The RM80 was produced from 1977 to 2001, when it was replaced by the RM85. As part of a joint effort with Kawasaki from 2003-2006, Suzuki was able to add 2-stroke RM60, RM65 and RM100 models to its lineup for a brief time. These were all rebadged versions of the Kawasaki KX bikes of the same size. Also in the 2000s, Suzuki started adding 4-stroke models to its youth lineup. While the DR-Z70, DR-Z110 and DR-Z125 don't have the exciting snap of their 2-stroke counterparts, they are still great machines for younger riders to learn to ride on.
