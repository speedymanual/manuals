Kawasaki Sport ATV Service Manuals


Kawasaki has been making sport ATVs since the late 1980s. In 1987 they offered the 4-stroke Mojave 110 and 250, and the 2-stroke Tecate 250, which was a competitor to Honda's TRX250R and Suzuki's QuadRacer 250. In 2003 Kawasaki joined the high-performance sport ATV market with the KFX 400, which was created as a rebaged Z400 in a joint effort with Suzuki. Kawasaki created a unique big bore sport machine in 2004 with the KFX 700. It featured a 700 v-twin motor with CVT transmission like you would find in a utility machine, but with sporty looks and 2wd. Kawasaki joined the race 450 ATV market in 2008 with the KFX 450R. Being a few years later to the 450 market than the rest of the manufacturers helped Kawasaki offer advanced features, like fuel injection and an alumninum frame. Kawasaki has been absent from the sport ATV market since 2015.
