Yamaha 3-Wheeler Service Manuals


Yamaha offered several different 3-wheeled models during the heyday of trikes in the 1980s. The Tri-Zinger 60 was a great model for the younger rider, while the Tri-Moto line of the 175, 200 and 225 offered a good mix of work and play. In 1985, Yamaha joined the high-performance sport trike crowd with the release of their Tri-Z 250, built to compete with Honda's ATC250R and Kawasaki's Tecate 3. By 1987 Yamaha had discontinued their 3-wheeled lineup because of the CSPC ban on new 3-wheeled machines.
