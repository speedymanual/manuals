Yamaha Sport ATV Service Manuals


Yamaha introduced their sport ATVs in the late 1980s. The 2-stroke Banshee and 4-stroke Warrior were both released in 1987, and each continued on nearly unchanged for 20 years. The 2-stroke Blaster was added the following year, and it also stuck around for 20 years with only minor cosmetic updates. Yamaha joined the 450 race ATV scene in 2004 with the introduction of the YFZ450. At the time it was the sport ATV that was closest to being race ready from the dealer. It featured a steel frame and carburetor. After not being made during the 2010 or 2011 model years, the YFZ450 was brought back in 2012 with minor updates. In 2009, the YFZ450R was introduced as a race-ready ATV. It featured 50" width for better handling, an alumninum frame and electronic fuel injection and is one of the few 450s still made today.
