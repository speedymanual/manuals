Honda Sport ATV Service Manuals


As the 3-wheeled Honda ATC line came to an end around 1986/1987, Honda was already transitioning into the 4-wheeled ATV market. In 1985/1986 Honda released the TRX70, TRX125, TRX200sx and legendary TRX250R, a 4-wheeled version of their also legendary ATC250R. The TRX250x followed in 1987, and it was replaced by the TRX300ex in 1993. The 300ex was a popular choice throughout the 1990s and 2000s. The performance sport ATV market stalled out in the 1990s, causing racers to begin building hybrid models, usually created by using a TRX250R frame with a 4-stroke motor. Honda took notice of this reingited the sport market in 1999 with the TRX400ex, a completely new machine that borrowed a motor from Honda's XR400 dirt bike and frame geometry from the TRX250R. The 400ex was a huge success, and can be attributed with bringing about the 450R class we know today. Honda released their own 450R in 2004, much to the delight of racers everywhere. Honda has been mostly absent from the sport ATV market as of late, now only offering a couple models that haven't been updated in years.
