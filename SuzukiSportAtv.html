Suzuki Sport ATV Service Manuals


Suzuki has been making their QuadSport/QuadRacer lineup of sport ATVs since the mid 1980s. The 230 and 250 models were popular 4-stroke models, but Suzuki lit up the performance sport scene with the LT-250R QuadRacer in 1985. Created as a direct competitor to the Honda TRX250R, the Suzuki was a favorite among racers. It continued on three more years after Honda discontinued their 250R. For riders wanting even more 2-stroke power than the 250R, Suzuki unleashed the QuadZilla 500 in 1987, and it was produced until 1990. They are still desirable today. From the mid 1990s into the early 2000s Suzuki mostly stayed out of the sport ATV market. Then in 2003 they came out swinging with the QuadSport LT-Z400, or Z400 for short. It was made once again to battle a popular Honda; this time it was the TRX400ex. Unlike the 250R battle which was decidedly won by Honda, many would argue that Suzuki won the 400 battle with their twin-cam, liquid cooled machine with reverse. In 2006 Suzuki went back to the drawing board to create the next QuadRacer, this time a race ready 450R, complete with fuel injection and a track-ready stance. Unfortunately the 450R only lasted four short years before being discontinued by Suzuki, and hasn't been seen since 2009. Suzuki has been absent from the sport ATV market since 2014 with the exception of their youth 50 and 90 models.
