Suzuki RM 2-Stroke Dirt Bike Service Manuals


Suzuki was a major player in the 2-stroke MX scene from the early days in the 1970s until the end in the early 2000s. The RM80 was a popular choice for young riders until it was replaced by the RM85 which remains a popular choice for early teen riders who aren't yet ready for a 125 or 250F. The RM125 and RM250 have been around since about the beginning of MX racing, and were finally phased out after the 2008 model year in favor of the 4-stroke MX models. In another joint effort with Kawasaki, there were even RM60 and RM65 models available for even younger riders. These were rebaged versions of Kawasaki's KX60 and KX65.
