Honda CR 2-Stroke Dirt Bike Service Manuals


Honda introduced the legendary CR line of MX bikes way back in the 1970s in the early days of motocross. A youth CR60R was made for two short years in the 1980s, and was replaced by the CR80R, which eventually became the CR85R, and is still a favorite for early teens. The venerable CR125R and CR250R were there from the beginning and each hung around until 2007, when they were finally phased out in favor of the 4-stroke MX bikes. In the early 1980s the CR480R was the open class king, and it was replaced in 1985 by the CR500R, which lasted until the 2001 model year.
