Arctic Cat Snowmobile Service Manuals


Arctic Cat has been producing snowmobiles since the early days of the sport, but many would argue they didn't become a major player in the market until the 1980s/1990s. The venerable ZL/ZR/ZRT lines were introduced in the mid 1990s with several differnt models available ranging in motor sizes from 440cc to 800cc, including several with electronic fuel injection (EFI). These were sleds designed and built by Arctic Cat but with 2-stroke motors manufactured by Suzuki, who does not make snowmobiles of their own. Throughout the rest of the 1990s and into the early 2000s the ZL/ZR/ZRT lines remained popular with buyers. Around 2002 Arctic Cat began experimenting with 4-stroke snowmobile models, and by 2004 the FireCat/Crossfire lines had replaced the ZL/ZR/ZRT line with the exception of the ZR900. By 2009 the FireCat/Crossfire line was discontinued, and was replaced by the C/F/M/XF lines, and the number of available 4-stroke models had increased significantly. In 2014 some familiar Arctic Cat sled names returned, with models like the ZR line and El Tigre being added to the lineup. Around this same time, Arctic Cat began parterning with Yamaha in order to be able to use their 4-stroke motors. Much like their previous relationship with Suzuki, Arctic Cat provides the chassis while Yamaha manufactures the motors. The difference this time around is Yamaha is a snowmobile manufacturer also, and they gained access to some of Arctic Cat chassis as part of the deal.
