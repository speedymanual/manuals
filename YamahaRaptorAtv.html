Yamaha Raptor ATV Service Manuals


Yamaha put the sport ATV market on notice in 2001 with the release of their Raptor 660. At the time it was the largest sport ATV motor ever released, and only larger when it was increased to 700cc in 2006. The Raptor 350 was introduced in 2004 as a replacement for the Warrior 350, which was being discontinued. While the Raptor 350 was marketed as a new model, it was really the Warrior wearing Raptor-style fenders. The Raptor line grew larger with the addition of smaller models in the 2010s. The 125 and 250 models were added, and they were unique in that they shared the same chassis but had different size motors for different levels of riders. They also are some of the only small-bore sport ATVs to offer a full manual clutch. There have also been different youth Raptor models available over the years, including 50, 80 and 90 models.
