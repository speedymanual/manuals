Kawasaki Youth ATV Service Manuals


Kawasaki entered the youth ATV market in 1987 with the Mojave 110, a smaller version of their Mojave 250, which was introduced the same year. After two years the Mojave 110 was discontinued, and Kawasaki was strangely absent from the youth ATV market throughout the 1990s. As part of a joint effort with Suzuki from 2003 to 2006, Kawasaki was able to add the KFX50 and KFX80 models to its lineup. These models were rebadged versions of the already well-established Suzuki LTA-50 and LT80 2-stroke youth ATVs. In 2007 Kawasaki introduced their own KFX50 and KFX90 models, each now being 4-strokes.
