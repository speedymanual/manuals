Kawasaki KX 2-Stroke Dirt Bike Service Manuals


Kawasaki's 2-stoke motocross KX line has been around since the 1970s. The KX125 and KX250 were the models with the longest run, finally ending after the 2007 model year. The KX60 was added as a youth model, and became a popular choice. In the 2000s it became the KX65. Kawasaki is unique in that it has offered not only the KX80 and KX85, but also has the KX100 in its lineup for riders that have mastered the 80/85 but are not yet ready for the full sized KX125. New models of the 65, 85 and 100 are still available today, making Kawasaki the only Japanese manufacturer still making youth-sized 2-stroke bikes. Like Honda, Kawasaki used to offer a beastly 500cc 2-stroke bike, the KX500 made from 1988 to 2004. Kawasaki also offered trail-oriented 2-stoke bikes, the KDX line. The main differences from the KX to the KDX line were the additions of a kickstand and headlight, and different transmission gearing more suited to trail riding. These models were offered in 200, 220 and 250 models.
